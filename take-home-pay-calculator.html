What Is Take-Home Pay?

Take-home pay is the amount of money you keep after taxes are taken out of your gross income. Gross pay is the total amount earned before taxes, while net pay, also called take-home pay, is what remains after deductions such as income tax.

This calculator gives a simple estimate using the tax rate you enter. It is useful for understanding paycheck value, comparing gross and net income, and planning your weekly, monthly, or yearly budget.

Take-Home Pay Formula

This calculator uses a simple net pay formula.

Estimated Taxes = Gross Income × (Tax Rate ÷ 100)
Take-Home Pay = Gross Income − Estimated Taxes

Take-Home Pay Calculator FAQ

What is take-home pay?

Take-home pay is the amount of money you keep after taxes and deductions are removed from your gross income.

What is the difference between gross pay and net pay?

Gross pay is your total earnings before deductions. Net pay, or take-home pay, is the amount left after taxes and other deductions.

Is this calculator exact?

No. This is a simplified estimate based on the tax rate you enter. Actual paycheck deductions may vary by state, employer, benefits, and other factors.

Can I use this for weekly, monthly, or yearly income?

Yes. You can choose weekly, monthly, or annual income and estimate net pay for that period.

Why is take-home pay important for budgeting?

Budgeting with net pay is more realistic because it shows the amount you may actually have available to spend or save after taxes.

Can I use this calculator for job comparisons?

Yes. It is helpful for comparing jobs or offers when you want to understand estimated after-tax income instead of just gross salary.
