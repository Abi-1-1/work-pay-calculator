How to Use This Budget Calculator

Choose whether you want to plan your budget by week or by month. Then select how you earn your income: hourly, weekly, or monthly. Enter your income first, then add your expenses using the same time period.

After calculation, the tool shows your total income, total expenses, money left, category percentages, recommended budget targets, daily spending suggestions, and a simple 50 / 30 / 20 budget view.

What Each Budget Category Means

Housing / Rent

Rent, mortgage, housing payments, and major living costs.

Food / Groceries

Groceries, basic meals, and regular food spending.

Transport

Fuel, bus, train, taxi, car maintenance, and work travel costs.

Utilities

Electricity, water, internet, phone bills, and similar monthly services.

Loans / Debt

Credit cards, loan payments, debt minimums, and repayment obligations.

Emergency Fund / Savings

Money set aside for financial safety, unexpected costs, or future planning.

Personal / Wants

Entertainment, hobbies, shopping, coffee, subscriptions, and flexible spending.

Other

Anything that does not fit the main categories above.

Simple Budget Tips

  • Try to keep housing costs near 25%–30% of income when possible.
  • Build an emergency fund even if you start with a small amount.
  • If your spending is above 80% of income, review personal and flexible expenses first.
  • If you have debt, track minimum payments separately and avoid missing due dates.
  • Use this calculator regularly to compare your real spending to your target budget.

Quick FAQ

Is this budget calculator weekly or monthly?

It can work as both. You can choose weekly or monthly mode at the top of the page. Just make sure your income and expenses use the same period.

Can I use hourly income?

Yes. Select hourly income, enter your hourly rate, and add your weekly work hours. The calculator will estimate the correct weekly or monthly income for your budget.

Are the recommended percentages exact financial rules?

No. They are simple planning guidelines. Real budgets depend on rent, city, debt, family size, and income level.

What does budget health status mean?

Healthy means your spending is more balanced. Warning means your expenses are taking most of your income. Overspending means your expenses are too high for the selected period.
